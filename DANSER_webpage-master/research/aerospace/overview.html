Aerospace

Our Lab conducts research in two major categories of Aerospace engineering: Aeroelastic Control, and Guidance and Navigation

Aeroelastic control focuses on the active control of fluid-structure interactions. This is especially important for aerospace applications where energy and environmental cost motivate mass reductions resulting in more flexible and compliant aircraft. We focus primarily on a subset of aeroelastic control that is used for morphing wings.

Guidance and navigation are the general engineering area of determining the state of the vehicle and the next path it should take. Our research has a particular focus on payload directed flight, which is the determination of waypoint selection based on the payload of the vehicle, and navigation in GPS-denied environments.

Current Projects - Aeroelastic Control:

  • Aeroelastic Control of Lattice-based Structures
  • Active Tip Twist for Blended Body Wings

Current Projects - Guidance and Navigation:

  • Distributed Sampling Using Small Unmanned Aerial Vehicals for Scientific Missions
  • Assistive Living Robot
  • RF Localization for GPS-Denied Environments
  • RF Network Localization Method for Unmanned Robotics
